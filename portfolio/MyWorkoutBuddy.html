My Workout Buddy

A concept for a fitness app.

Project Overview

My Workout Buddy is a fitness app designed to help users track their workouts, set fitness goals, and stay motivated through personalized workout plans, progress tracking, and social features. This concept was developed by a team of six over 12 weeks. We chose to pursue a fitness app because it is a widely used app category, and through our initial research we identified several gaps in existing solutions that we felt could be meaningfully improved upon. My contributions included conducting user research, creating wireframnes, and developing the final solution.

The Problem

Many fitness apps on the market are either too complex for beginners, lack desired features, rely on frustrating microtransactions, or deliver a poor user experience. Users often struggle to find an app that genuinely meets their needs and keeps them motivated. The goal of this project was to design a fitness app that is intuitive, feature-rich, and accessible to users of all fitness and digital literacy levels, while providing an overall positive and engaging experience.

Ideation

Over the first five to six weeks, we brainstormed concepts and evaluated the feasibility of each. We settled on a sleek, modern, and user-friendly design direction, with an emphasis on familiarity and ease of use for users of all fitness and technical backgrounds. To better understand the needs and pain points of potential users, we defined three core tasks that a user would need to complete within the app and developed a low-fidelity prototype to test them. We then iterated into a high-fidelity prototype, which was used to conduct user research with participants completing the same tasks.

The Tasks

  • Task 1: The user is onboarding; they create an account, input their physical attributes as well as their desired goals.
  • Task 2: The user works with the app to develop a personalized workout plan for a specific muscle group (or multiple, depending on their needs).
  • Task 3: The user logs their workout sets/reps and amount of weight to track their progress overtime.

User Research

To understand the needs and pain points of potential users, we conducted surveys and usability tests. We gathered feedback on our high fideilty prototype and used it to make improvements to the final design. These are the feedback we gathered on each task from the user research:

  • Task 1:
    • Users felt the onboarding process was helpful and mostly intuitive.
    • Some of the labels and color choices cause confusion.
    • Overall, an effective process but has potential to cause confusion or frustration without support.
  • Task 2:
    • Participants found the presets to be helpful, especially beginners.
    • Having access to predefined lists of exercises was very useful, videos and demos help users feel comfortable.
    • There was some confusion with the filtering system and structure of the preset pages.
  • Task 3:
    • Users appreciated how the system takes you step by step to track your workout, so you do not miss anything
    • Users like the option to rate your workout and write notes based on your experience
    • One user thought the workout history section was very clear but basic
    • Users thought having a confirmation screen after tracking your workout was helpful
